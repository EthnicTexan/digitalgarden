# Mexican Texas Era
---
The era from **1821 to 1836** is known as the **Mexican National Era** and the **Texas Revolution**. It is arguably the most transformative 15 years in Texas history, shifting the region from a sparsely populated Spanish province to an independent republic.
#### This era spans from **1821 to 1836**

# 1821
---
>**The Empresario System (1821–1830):** After Mexico won independence from Spain in 1821, the new government realized they needed more people to buffer against Indigenous raids and U.S. expansion.
>
>* **Stephen F. Austin:** Carrying out his father's plan, he brought the "Old Three Hundred" families to Texas.
>* **The Deal:** Settlers received cheap land in exchange for becoming Mexican citizens, obeying Mexican laws, learning Spanish, and converting to Catholicism.
>* **The Boom:** The population skyrocketed as Americans (mostly from the Southern U.S.) moved in, bringing their culture, language, and the institution of slavery with them.

# 1830
---
>**The Law of April 6, 1830:** Alarmed by the influx of Americans, Mexico banned further immigration from the U.S., placed taxes on imports, and abolished slavery—though the law was difficult to enforce.

# 1834
---
 >**Centralism vs. Federalism:** Antonio López de **Santa Anna** rose to power and scrapped the democratic Mexican Constitution of 1824, declaring himself a dictator. This angered both Anglo-settlers and many local Tejanos (Texans) who wanted local control.
