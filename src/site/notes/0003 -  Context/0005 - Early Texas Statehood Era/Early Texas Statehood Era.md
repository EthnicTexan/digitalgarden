#### This era spans from **1845 - 1861**
#### Click here for the prior [[0003 -  Context/0004 - Republic Of Texas Era/Republic Of Texas Era\|Republic Of Texas Era]]

# 1845
---
>**Annexation Finalized:** Texas officially joins the United States on December 29. President Anson Jones hands over the government to the first state governor, James Pinckney Henderson.


# 1846
---
> **The Mexican-American War Begins:** Disputes over the Rio Grande boundary lead to the first shots of the war on the Texas-Mexico border. Many Texans, including the Texas Rangers, enlist to fight.


# 1847
---
> **Victory in Mexico:** U.S. forces, bolstered by Texas volunteers, capture Mexico City. General Winfield Scott’s campaign effectively ends the military phase of the conflict.


# 1848
---
> **The Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo:** Mexico officially cedes its claims to Texas and the Southwest. The Rio Grande is permanently established as the southern border of Texas.


# 1849
---
> **The Gold Rush Trail:** Texas becomes a major thoroughfare for "Forty-Niners" heading to California. New frontier forts are established to protect settlers from Comanche and Kiowa raids.


# 1850
---
> **The Compromise of 1850:** Texas settles its boundary dispute with New Mexico. In exchange for $10 million from the U.S. government to pay off old Republic debts, Texas gives up its claim to "Upper" New Mexico and the "Panhandle" strip.


# 1851
---
> **Frontier Defense:** The U.S. Army continues building a line of forts (the "Second Line") across the Texas Hill Country and West Texas to encourage western settlement.


# 1852
---
> **Steam and Steel:** The first railroad company in Texas, the Buffalo Bayou, Brazos, and Colorado Railway, begins construction, signaling the start of a transportation revolution.


# 1853
---
> **Political Growth:** The Democratic Party becomes the dominant political force in Texas. The state uses some of its $10 million windfall to fund internal improvements and education.


# 1854
---
>**Indigenous Reservations:** Texas establishes two small reservations for Native American tribes on the Brazos River, though these would only last a few years due to settler hostility.
# 1855
---
> **The Know-Nothing Challenge:** A secretive political party (the American Party) briefly challenges the Democrats in Texas, focusing on anti-immigrant and anti-Catholic rhetoric.


# 1856
---
> **The Camel Experiment:** In a strange military trial, the U.S. Army brings camels to Camp Verde, Texas, testing their ability to transport supplies across the arid West Texas deserts.
# 1857
---
> **The Cart War:** Violent clashes break out in South Texas between Anglo and Texan teamsters (freight haulers) over control of the profitable shipping routes from the coast.


# 1858
---
> **The Battle of Little Robe Creek:** Texas Rangers and allies attack a Comanche village in the heart of the Comancheria, marking a shift toward more aggressive frontier warfare.


# 1859
---
> **The Return of Sam Houston:** After being ousted from the U.S. Senate for his pro-Union views, Sam Houston is elected Governor of Texas, hoping to prevent secession.


# 1860
---
> **The Election of Lincoln:** Abraham Lincoln wins the U.S. Presidency without a single vote from Texas. "Texas Troubles"—a series of mysterious fires—spark fears of slave revolts across the state.
