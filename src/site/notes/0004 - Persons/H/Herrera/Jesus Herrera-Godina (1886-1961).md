# Jesus Herrera (1886–1961)

---

**Jesus Herrera** was a lifelong resident of South Texas, born in Floresville and established as a farmer in the Wilson County area. He raised a sprawling family in Poth and Floresville before spending his final years in San Antonio.

## 1886 - JAN 28 - BIRTH

---

### Location - Floresville, Wilson County, Texas

Jesus was born into the farming community of Floresville during the late 19th-century boom of the region.

* **Father:** [[0004 - Persons/H/Herrera/Jose María Herrera (1850–1908)\|Jose María Herrera (1850–1908)]]
* **Mother:** [[0004 - Persons/G/Godina/Juana Godina-Trevino (1862–1894)\|Juana Godina-Trevino (1862–1894)]] 

## 1900 - Household & Labor

---

### Location - Justice Precinct 1, Wilson, Texas

Following the death of his mother in 1894, Jesus is recorded at age 14 living with his father and siblings.

* **Occupation:** Day Laborer.
* **Status:** Single, living in his father’s rented household.

## 1905 - MARRIAGE - To [[0004 - Persons/G/Garza/Juanita Garza (1888–1943)\|Juanita Garza (1888–1943)]]

---

### Location - Floresville, Wilson County, Texas

Jesus married **Juanita Garza** (1888–1943) at age 19. This union would produce over a dozen children, many of whom remained in the Wilson County and San Antonio areas.

* **Spouse:** Juanita Garza
* **Family Note:** Juanita passed away in 1943, after which Jesus later remarried **Otillia Herrera** (recorded in the 1950 Census).

## 1917–1942 - Military Drafts & World Wars

---

### Location - Wilson County, Texas

Jesus’s physical description and residence are captured in both the WWI and WWII "Old Man's" draft registrations.

* **WWI (1917):** Described as tall and slender with dark hair and brown eyes.
* **WWII (1942):** Registered while living in Poth, Texas, listing "Mrs. Juanita Herrera" as his next of kin.

## 1910–1950 - Farming and Resilience

---

Jesus spent decades as a farmer in the Poth and Floresville areas, navigating the challenges of the Great Depression and the transition to mid-century industrial agriculture.

* **1910 Census:** Living in Wilson County with 3 children.
* **1930 Census:** Listed as a farmer on "Precinct 1" farm, owning his own account.
* **1950 Census:** Recorded at age 64 living on **Kosciusko Road** in Poth with his second wife, Otillia. "Kosciuszko Rd" appears to be the name of a local road in the general area of Poth, Texas, and the nearby small community of Kosciusko.

## 1961 - DEC 18 - DEATH

---

### Location - San Antonio, Bexar County, Texas

Jesus passed away at the age of 75 at his residence on **234 Patton St** in San Antonio.

* **Burial:** Harper Cemetery, Poth, Wilson County, Texas.
* **Legacy:** He was buried in the community where he spent the majority of his working life.
